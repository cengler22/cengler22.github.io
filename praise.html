Worthiness of Emerson's Praise

Emerson's
quote, "The great man is he who in the midst of the crowd keeps with perfect sweetness the independence of solitude" neither pertains to or contrasts from Nick's actions. Nick does not meet Emerson's definition of an
outsider
in this situation because he is not intentionally removing himself from the others emotionally or physically. Nick's character traits, being scared by normal sounds in the
woods
shows cowardice, not a differentiation from society that's intentionally made to stand out. Nick is closer to the antithesis of Emerson's concept of an outsider because Nick doesn't wish to be an outsider, he instead wishes to be like the others and fit in. He doesn't want to embrace what makes him unique, which was what Emerson strived to do.
